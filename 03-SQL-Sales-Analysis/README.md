# 🗄️ Music Store Database Analysis

## 📌 Project Overview

This project involved analyzing a Chinook-style digital music store database using SQL.

The project included business questions ranging from basic to advanced levels and required the use of SQL joins, aggregate functions, subqueries, Common Table Expressions (CTEs), and window functions.

---

## 🎯 Project Objective

The objectives were to:

- Analyze employee information.
- Study customer purchasing behavior.
- Analyze invoice data.
- Identify popular music genres.
- Analyze artists and tracks.
- Compare sales across countries and cities.
- Answer business questions using SQL.

---

## 📂 Database

The relational database contained **11 linked tables**:

- Employee
- Customer
- Invoice
- Invoice Line
- Track
- Album
- Artist
- Genre
- Media Type
- Playlist
- Playlist Track

The database contained:

- **614 invoices**
- **4,757 invoice lines**
- **3,503 tracks**

### Advanced

Identify the most popular music genre for each country using a CTE and window function.

---

## 🔑 Key Insights

- **Mohan Madan**, Senior General Manager at Level L7, was identified as the most senior employee.
- The **USA** generated the highest number of invoices with 131 invoices.
- Canada and Brazil followed with 76 and 61 invoices respectively.
- **Prague** generated the highest total invoice amount by city at approximately **€273.24**.
- **František Wichterlová** was the highest-spending customer with total purchases of approximately **€144.54**.
- **Led Zeppelin, U2, and Deep Purple** were the top three Rock artists by track count.
- Rock was the most-purchased genre across several major markets, including the USA, Canada, France, Brazil, Germany, and the UK.
- The average track name length was approximately **15.9 characters**.
