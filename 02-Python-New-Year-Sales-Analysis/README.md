# 🐍 New Year Sales – Exploratory Data Analysis

## 📌 Project Overview

This project presents an end-to-end Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) of a New Year sales dataset using Python.

The analysis focused on understanding customer demographics and purchasing behavior to identify who was purchasing, what they were purchasing, and which customer segments contributed most to sales.

---

## 🎯 Project Objective

The objectives of this project were to:

- Clean and prepare the sales dataset.
- Analyze customer demographics.
- Study purchasing behavior.
- Identify high-performing customer segments.
- Analyze product category performance.
- Generate visual insights from the data.

---

## 📂 Dataset

The dataset contained:

- **11,251 records**
- **15 columns**

Important fields included:

- User ID
- Customer Name
- Product ID
- Gender
- Age Group
- Age
- Marital Status
- State
- Zone

## 🧹 Data Preparation

The following steps were performed:

- Loaded the CSV dataset using Pandas.
- Used `cp1252` encoding to handle special characters.
- Inspected data types.
- Checked missing values.
- Checked duplicate records.
- Removed irrelevant placeholder/index columns.
- Grouped data using Pandas operations.

---

## 📊 Analysis Performed

The analysis covered:

- Gender-wise sales.
- Age-group analysis.
- Marital-status analysis.
- State-wise order analysis.
- Occupation-wise sales.
- Product-category analysis.

---

## 🔑 Key Insights

- Female customers represented approximately **70% of the records** and contributed significantly higher sales than male customers.
- The **26–35 age group** was the largest customer segment and also the highest-spending group.
- Unmarried customers contributed higher total spending than married customers in the dataset.
- **Uttar Pradesh, Maharashtra, and Karnataka** were among the top states by order count.
- The **IT sector** was the highest-spending occupation group.
- The **Food** category generated the highest total sales, while Clothing & Apparel recorded the highest order count.
- The difference between order count and total spending suggests variations in average purchase value across product categories.

---

## 💡 Business Takeaways

The findings suggest that marketing campaigns could focus strongly on the 26–35 customer segment and female customers, who represented a significant share of purchasing activity.

The analysis also indicates opportunities to target high-spending occupational groups and promote product categories with higher average basket values.
